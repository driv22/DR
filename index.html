My Journey

26 years. One constant:
making technology work.

From field technician to Microsoft AI — a career built on hands-on execution, relentless learning, and a genuine obsession with what technology can do for people.

~2000 – Early Career
Field Technology Agent
Charter Communications · Geek Squad

Started from the ground up as a field agent — troubleshooting, installing, and repairing technology in the real world. Built an instinct for diagnosing complex problems fast and a relentless drive to find solutions that actually stick.

~2003 – Leadership
Field Operations Manager
Charter Communications

Stepped into management, leading a team of field agents across service operations. Discovered a passion for developing people and building high-performing teams — a leadership mindset that has shaped every role since.

~2006 – Finance Sector
IT Support Specialist · L1–L3
Chopper Trading

Entered the high-stakes world of financial services technology — providing Level 1–3 support in a trading environment where every second matters. Gained deep respect for the performance and reliability demands of finance.

~2009 – Enterprise Infrastructure
Implementation Engineer
Technology Solutions Group

Led enterprise deployments spanning wireless networks, data networking, Microsoft Lync Server, Skype for Business Server, and complex Avaya & Microsoft UC integrations — building the infrastructure foundation that would define the rest of my career.

~2015 – Enterprise Scale
Senior Engineer · Unified Communications
Allstate Insurance

Led the enterprise-wide migration from Webex to Skype for Business, followed by a full Microsoft Teams implementation — replacing legacy systems, decommissioning aging infrastructure, and transforming how one of America's largest insurers communicates and collaborates.

~2020 – Microsoft
Solutions Engineer · Microsoft 365 Copilot Focus
Microsoft

Joined Microsoft as a Solutions Engineer with a dedicated focus on Microsoft 365 Copilot — helping financial institutions understand, adopt, and scale Copilot across their organizations. From demos and discovery to architecture and deployment strategy, this was where years of enterprise experience met the next era of AI.
